Benjamin Franklin - The man we all should look up to in 21st century.

How was he like ?

  • He would delight the person he meet,full of humour -- making him naturally sociable.
  • He was dutiful and affectionate towards his friends and family -- kept in contact with them, wrote letters till the end of life from wherever he was.
    Be slow in choosing a friend, slower in changing.
  • He ran away from his family, penniless. Expressed his ideas by starting a news paper and became friends with reminders.
  • He was obsessed with self-improvement and had practical organization skills
  • His course of action was strategic and had persuasive manner of speaking.

Take Away 1: His Journaling Skill.

Why should we journal?

Thinking in our mind is mostly like writing with a vapouring ink. Our course of daily actions are dictated by our thoughts --> which in terms are dictated by the environment and our past experiences. Our environment keeps streaming gigabits of data to our sensors, which our mind keeps processing. If we live like this it will be like -- Living on a daily basis, solving same problems everyday.. While it is necessary and unavoidable, to value ourself in human society we should achieve difficult goals -- which is a long time affair involving consistency,self-discipline. We should have a rough idea where we want to be after a month,quarter,year and decade, of course we course correct along the way but we will reach somewhere near to that rough idea of ours

So in this data driven world -- we should have the data of our past thoughts,emotions and actions, so that we can update ourself accordingly -- avoiding common pitfalls, automating daily rituals aka. self-disciplining ourself We must do some version of following things.

What we learned from his journals?

  • Where to focus on what time ? Breaking day into larger chunks of time and focusing on set of actions to do one larger task ---> Actions to perform tasks --> Tasks to reach goals.
  • Improve your systems i.e the way of doing things -- so it can be reapplied to similar actions.
  • Instead of mastering one skill in short period of time -- from big picture break it down into modules and proceed step by step on daily basis till mastery. After that find another endower.
  • Self reflect on ourself -- Why we worry? What valuable work I'm performing ? What are the motives behind my actions ?
  • What good shall I do this day?
